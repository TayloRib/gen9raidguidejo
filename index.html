Six Star Raid Strategies

Pokémon (Jolteon - Orthworm)

Jolteon (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Tera Blast:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
This moves type becomes the same as their Tera Type
Thunderbolt:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 10% chance of paralyzing the target with the exception of Electric types
Shadow Ball:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 20% chance of lowering the targets Special Defense by one stage
Thunder Wave:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 90%
Paralyzes the target unless they are Ground or Electric type
Calm Mind:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the users Special Attack and Special Defense stats by one stage each
Electric Terrain:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Electric moves used by affected Pokémon by 30%
  • - Prevents affected Pokémon from being afflicted by sleep or Yawn
  • - Boosts the highest stat of a Pokémon with Quark Drive by 30% or 50% if it's the Speed Stat
Abilities:Back to Top
Volt Absorb:Category: Status
When hit by an Electric move its HP is restored by 1/4th its maximum HP
Quick Feet:Category: Status
Boosts the speed stat if this Pokémon has a status condition

Tips:
    The main focus you should have in a fight against Jolteon are its Tera Type, and its ability to use stat altering and status moves to deal huge damage.
  • - Tera Blast uses the type advantages of its Tera Type to deal special effective damage, so you need to worry about more type disadvantages than Water, Flying, Ghost, Psychic, or Fighting
  • - Electric Terrain will boost Thunderbolt Base STAB damage to 120
  • - Calm Mind will boost a moveset that tears through your health
Pokémon with Taunt are great here to prevent Electric Terrain, Calm mind, and Thunderwave but even just having Ice Spinner, Steel Roller, or Defog will help a lot
Overall Jolteon is a pretty weak Pokémon, putting the majority of its power into Special Attack. A strong Physical Attacker here will make quick work of its low Defense and Health, but if you're playing support definitely make sure you have a High Special Defense.

Kilowattrel (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Hurricane:Category: Special / Power: 110 / Accuracy: 70%
Has a 30% chance of confusing the target, always hits during Rain
Thunder:Category: Special / Power: 110 / Accuracy: 70%
Has a 10% chance of paralyzing the target with the exception of Electric types, always hits during Rain
Uproar:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
The Pokémon is locked into this move for 3 turns unless interupted, effected Pokémon will wake up and be unable to sleep (with the exception of Ghost Pokémon that are not effected)
Scary Face:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Lowers the targets Speed stat by two stages
Charge:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
The next time the user does an Electric move its power will be doubled; this also raises the users Special Defense by one stage
Rain Dance:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Water type moves used by affected Pokémon by 50%
  • - Reduces the power of Fire type moves used by affected Pokémon by 50%
Abilities:Back to Top
Wind Power:Category: Status
When hit by a wind move it becomes charged, boosting the power of the next Electric move it uses
Volt Absorb:Category: Status
When hit by an Electric move its HP is restored by 1/4th its maximum HP
Competitive:Category: Status
When a stat of a Pokémon with this Ability is lowered by an opponent, its Special Attack is increased by two stages for each stat effected

Tips:
    With help from Charge and Rain Dance, Kilowattrels moves Hurricane and Thunder are super formidable
  • - Charge will Double Thunders damage to 220 Base STAB Damage
  • - Rain Dance will guarantee that Hurricane and Thunder hit every time
  • - If a Kilowattrel with Competitive is hit by a stat lowering move, all of its damage dealing moves get a two stat damage boost
If you do not bring a Pokémon with Taunt, or Sunny Day to prevent Rain Dance you will suffer. Both of these moves have added benefits: Taunt will eliminate additional moves like Scary Face, Charge, and Rain Dance; and Sunny day will lower the accuracy of Hurricane and Thunder to 50%.
A really good option here for coverage is an Electric type with Lightning Rod, this will draw in Kilowattrels Thunder and prevent Charge from being an issue as well. It will also prevent unaware teammates from using electric moves to Charge Wind Power. Just be sure not to use an electric move yourself as you'll end up healing Kilowattrel if it has Volt Absorb.
Note, be sure to avoid any move with stat lowering effects or secondary effects as it will boost the damage of a Kilowattrel that has Competitive.
The Defense and Special Defense of this Pokémon are both very low, if you prepare for this fight and bring an attacker with High Special Defense you'll have no problems.

Supreme Overlord:Category: Status

Tips:
    Because of Kingambits dark/steel typing, it is resistant or immune to about 60% of move types, and with Swords Dance, Defiant, and Night Slash it has the potential for huge damage.
  • - Defiant will raise its Attack stat by two stages for every attempt to lower its stats
  • - Swords Dance will raise its Attack stat by two stages every use
  • - Night Slash has a higher chance to Critcal hit, bypasing stat boosts and Reflect to deal huge damage
Fortunately, each of Kingambits moves don't do a lot of damage on their own, and moves like Taunt can go a long way to prevent Swords Dance and Thunder Wave.
Kingambit does have a huge base Attack and Defense, but it balances this with a low Special Defense, and because it's 4X weak to Fighting moves, Aura Sphere will do massive damage and make quick work of this Raid.

Tips:
    Because of Klawfs diverse moveset, it's super effective to about 60% of enemy types. Combination moves like Rock Smash and Stone Edge will do huge Attack Damage, and between Sandstorm and Iron Defense you're going to have a hard time getting any significant damage on this Pokémon.
  • - Rock Smash has a 50% chance of lowering your Defense
  • - Stone Edge has a higher chance to Critcal hit, bypasing stat boosts and Reflect to deal huge damage
  • - Iron Defense raises its Deffense by two stages
  • - Sandstorm raises its Special Defense by 50% during its duration
Pokémon with Taunt are super helpful here to stop Klawf from using Iron Defense and Sandstorm. Additionally, Fighting Types will get extra protection from Stone Edge and Knock Off.
Klawf has huge Attack and Defense, but grately suffers from its low Special Defense. A special Attacker with High Defense will do best here.

Overall Leafeon is a pretty weak Pokémon. Its moveset isn't particularly dangerous and it has very low Special Defense and Health. If you're not careful though, its Attack will do huge damage, so be sure to bring a Special Attacker with High Defense.

Has a 20% chance of paralyzing, freezing, or burning the target with the exception of causing ailments to their respective types

Paralyzes the target with the exception of electric types
Iron Defense:Category: Physical / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the users Defense stat by two stages
Electric Terrain:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Electric moves used by affected Pokémon by 30%
  • - Prevents affected Pokémon from being afflicted by sleep or Yawn

Magnet Pull:Category: Status
Does not effect raid
Sturdy:Category: Status
Does not effect raid
Analytic:Category: Status
If this Pokémon executes its move after all other Pokémon have made their move, the power of its move is increased by 30%

Tips:
    Magnezone has an intimidating list of attacks that have huge damage potential if Rain Dance and Electric Terrain are used in combination
  • - Rain Dance seems strange because Magnezone has no Water moves to increase its damage, however it will make Thunder 100% accurate, and it will lower damage from Fire moves
  • - Electric Terrain will increase the damage Thunder does from 110 STAB base damage to ~ 146 STAB base
  • - Thunder Wave has the potential to Paralyze preventing you from attacking
  • - Flash Canon lowers your special defense making all of its moves do additional damage
Taunt is mandatory here to prevent Thunder Wave, Iron Defense, Electric Terrain, and Rain Dance. If you don't have taunt you're looking at two move slots in the form of Sunny Day to prevent Rain Dance and either Grassy Terrain, Misty Terrain, Psychic Terrain, Ice Spinner, or Steel Roller to prevent Electric Terrain. Unfortuantely these other moves will not stop Iron Defense or Thunder Wave which is why Taunt is so valueable.
Additionally Electric types are great for Defense here, especially if they have Lightning Rod which will remove Thunder from being an issue entirely, it also has the ability to increase your Special Attack damage with each strike. And Electric types are immune to Thunder Wave and paralyzing effects from Tri Attack.
Magnezone has both great Defense and decent Special Defense, it's only true weakness is its low HP and low Speed (which if utilized under the ability Analytic will increase Thunder from ~ 146 STAB base to 190). Bring a Special Attacker with high Special Defense and get this raid done quickly to stand a chance at winning. Note that by bringing a Special Attacker you're also avoiding its status raising move Iron Defense.

Tidy Up if used successfully against a heavy opponent has some good potential for damage, but bringing an attacker with Taunt will reduce that threat.
Mausholds usefulness isn't really felt in single battles like Raids, it has low Attack, Health, Defense, and Special Defense. If you bring a Special Attacker you'll avoid secondary effects from Play Rough and Charm and make quick work of this Raid.

Mimikyu is not a very intimidating Raid opponent, it has one Ability which doesn't have much impact on the fight, its overall moveset is easy to negate, and it has low overall Stats.
A good tip is to understand that if your first damage dealing move against Mimikyu is meant to hit hard, it will be negated by Disguise. Have a partner use a less effective move to break the Disguise first. Bringing a pokemon with Taunt is also useful here to prevent Mimikyu from increasing its Attack damage from Swords Dance.
If you bring an Attacker with High Defense to this fight, you'll do well. You might have to contend with lower Attack if Play Rough activates its 10% secondary attack, but thats pretty unlikely.

Strong Against:
Moves:
Iron Head:Category: Physical / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 30% chance of causing the target to flinch
Earthquake:Category: Physical / Power: 100 / Accuracy: 100%
Has no secondary effects

If the target is Flying type, has the Ability Levitate, or is under the effect of Magnet Rise or Telekinesis, this move causes that target to become vulnerable to Ground moves
Coil:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the users Attack, Defense, and Accuracy stats by one stage each
Sandstorm:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - This move will last for 5 turns, while in effect, all Pokémon who are neither Steel, Rock, nor Ground type will be damaged 1/16th of their maximum HP at the end of each turn
  • - All Rock Pokémon have their Special Defense raised by 50% for the duration of the sandstorm
Abilities:Back to Top
Earth Eater:Category: Status
When hit by a Ground move, its HP is restored by 1/4th of its maximum HP, and the move will have no effect
Sand Veil:Category: Status
Boosts evasiveness in a sandstorm

Tips:
Orthworm is annoying to fight due to its insanely high Defense and Sandstorm which will raise its Special Defense almost as high.
Because this Raid has the potential to last many turns, if you do not stop Orthworm from using Coil you will not win; this is why its important to bring a Pokémon with Taunt. Taunt will also prevent Orthworm from using Sandstorm, raising its Special Defense.
Note that because Orthworm also has Smack Down, if you're planning on bringing an attacker with Levitate, Magnet Rise, or Telekinesis your ability will be negated.
Bring a Special Attacker regardless of potential stat buffs. Having high Defense is helpful, but ultimately if you cannot prevent Orthworm from raising its Attack; your Defense will mean nothing. Stop Coil and Sandstorm, and make quick work of this Raid to win.
